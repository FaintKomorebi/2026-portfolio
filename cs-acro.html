Overview
01 — Context

90 years of expertise, buried in the wrong structure.

ACRO Automation Systems is a family-owned automation company specializing in custom assembly line machinery for industries ranging from automotive and medical devices to heavy equipment. Despite a strong reputation built over decades, their website had grown into a sprawling 29-page structure — deeply nested, visually outdated, and misaligned with the company they'd become.

The build had to live in WordPress, managed by ACRO's existing SEO team. The new design needed to honor the family-owned identity while meeting the expectations of modern industrial buyers — not a rebrand, a realignment.

The brief Modernize the site UI around new brand guidelines, reduce complexity, and build a platform that scales with ACRO's future.
The real problem The structure itself was the problem. 29 pages of nested, outdated content was actively hiding ACRO's credibility from the buyers they needed to reach.
Success looked like A leaner site that felt as engineered as ACRO's machines — and measurably moved buyer engagement.

Decision
02 — The Key Decision
"Analytics told us where buyers were going. We stopped making them work to get there."

Google Analytics showed ACRO's solutions section was the most visited part of the existing site — buyers were actively seeking it out despite the friction. The conventional approach would have kept it buried inside a navigation menu. Instead, we pulled it directly onto the homepage as the first scroll after the hero, fully clickable and organized by industry.

It was a deliberate structural break from how the old site was organized, and not everyone agreed with it immediately. But the data removed the debate — if we knew where buyers wanted to go, removing the steps between them and that content was the clearest call we could make.

Considered Keep solutions on a dedicated interior page Standard structure, but added a click and a page load between interest and information.
Considered Lead with a full-page hero video like competitors Would have matched the landscape, not differentiated from it. Memorable doesn't follow the crowd.
Chosen Surface solutions on scroll-one of the homepage Analytics confirmed it was the most sought-after content. Reducing that friction became the central structural decision of the redesign.
