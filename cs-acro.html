Case Study 01 · ACRO Automation Systems · 2025

Redesigning a legacy brand for modern customers.

ACRO's 29-page website was burying a 90-year manufacturing reputation. A procurement manager landing on it had no fast way to confirm ACRO even served their industry. Most didn't stay long enough to find out.

Role Lead Designer
Timeline 6 months
Platform WordPress · handed to ACRO's SEO team post-launch
Outcome +28% session duration · 1.4× inquiry volume
01Context

Ninety years of expertise, buried in the wrong structure.

ACRO had built something real over 90 years: a reputation for precision manufacturing that larger competitors couldn't match on track record alone. What it hadn't built was a way to communicate that. A procurement lead evaluating ACRO against three competitors had maybe 90 seconds to decide whether they were worth a call. The site gave them no quick answer.

Competitors ran sites of 17 and 21 pages. ACRO had 29. The brief was officially a UI refresh, but its stated goals, fewer steps to content and solutions front and center, were structural. I treated it as an information architecture project.

The Brief

A UI refresh following new brand guidelines. Stated goals: fewer steps to content, solutions as the main focus.

The Real Problem

Pages were nested inside pages, hiding the content customers came for and making the site needlessly hard to navigate.

Constraints

The build had to live in WordPress and be manageable by ACRO's in-house SEO team after launch. No budget for new copy, so the redesign had to work with existing content.

Reaching a solution: zero required clicks

Solutions are reachable two ways without a single click: browsable on the homepage at first scroll, or visible immediately in the nav dropdown. The intermediate landing page is gone. A buyer can confirm ACRO works in their space before committing to anything.

03The Key Decision
Analytics showed me where customers were going. I stopped making them work to get there.

Google Analytics showed ACRO's solutions pages were the most visited content on the site. Automated Welding, Robotics, and Automated Assembly all sat in the top five. Customers were seeking them out despite the friction, reading them, and then hitting a dead end: none of those pages had a call to action. I pulled solutions directly onto the homepage as the first scroll after the hero, fully clickable and organized by industry.

It was a deliberate structural break from how the old site was organized, and leadership pushed back initially. I brought the analytics into the room and walked through exactly where customers were going and how many steps stood between them and that content. The data resolved it.

Throughout the process I ran internal review sessions with engineers and stakeholders who mirror the buyers evaluating ACRO. The question stayed simple: if you landed here trying to figure out whether ACRO can build what you need, does this page get you there? Every structural decision was tested against that lens before it was finalized.

Considered Keep solutions on a dedicated interior page The standard pattern, but it kept a click and a page load between customers and the most requested content on the site.
Considered Lead with a full-page hero video like competitors Every competitor leads with one. Matching them would have traded ACRO's identity for a pattern that answers no buyer question. I pushed against this in internal reviews.
Chosen Surface solutions on scroll one of the homepage Analytics confirmed solutions were the most sought-after content. Removing that friction became the central structural decision of the redesign.
04The Solution
01 · Industry-led entry point

Solutions are organized by industry rather than product category, because that is how a buyer frames the problem. Not "I need automated assembly" but "I'm in automotive, does ACRO work in my space?" The industry labels answer that before the customer has to ask.

02 · Consolidation over elimination

Pages artificially split across multiple destinations were merged into single scrollable layouts. The content didn't shrink. The distance to it did.

03 · Closing the loop

This is the screen where the buried-credibility problem ends: the first view where a customer sees industry relevance, product capability, and a contact path at once. No scroll required.

Before Dispensing Systems, nested two levels deep behind a landing page.
After The same content, one click from the index, ending in a call to action.
05Outcome
Source · Google Analytics +28% Average session duration

Post-launch vs. the prior baseline. Visitors were staying longer and actually reading: learning what ACRO builds and whether it fits their need.

Source · CRM · 90 days post-launch 1.4× Inquiry volume

Sales confirmed quality improved alongside volume. Prospects were referencing specific solution pages in their first emails.

What I'd Do Differently

I'd push harder on content strategy earlier. The structural decisions were sound, but several solution pages launched with copy held over from the old site. The refresh fixed the UX; the content problem is ongoing, and I should have scoped it explicitly rather than treating it as a background task.
